What Are Authentication Failures?

Authentication Failures occur when an application does not properly verify the identity of users. This category includes:

  • Allowing weak or well-known passwords
  • Storing passwords in plain text or with weak hashing
  • No protection against automated attacks (brute force, credential stuffing)
  • Missing rate limiting and account lockout mechanisms
  • Exposing sensitive information in error messages

Why Are They Dangerous?

Authentication failures can lead to:

  • 🔓 Full account takeovers
  • 💳 Financial fraud through compromised accounts
  • 📊 Data breaches exposing millions of user records
  • 🏢 Ransomware deployment through compromised admin accounts
  • ⚖️ Legal liability and reputational damage
